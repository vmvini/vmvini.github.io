Ruppells Griffon (Australia) (2016) - Full Remote


I was hired by RuppellsGriffon to work as a junior fullstack web developer, but more focused in the frontend development with angular js. I was in the final steps of my graduation course (System Analysis and Development), when I received an email with the job announcement.

Their business model is all about giving their clients business insights from geospatial data. Their common projects involves gathering geospacial data, analyzing it, and expose it through applications.

There I learnt how to build bigger frontend applications, keep tracking production environment through logs, study a variety of web technologies (Mapbox gl js, Google APIs, GeoJSON, MongoDB)

The project I was most excited to work on was one of their map applications on top of "Mapbox gl". Although the frontend app was cool, I was mostly interested in the background tools I had to create to process raw geospatial data from different sources, so I had to organize that variety of data, extract points, geometries, convert them to GeoJSON. Also I had to build crawlers to gather data from different sites, including the site of the government of Australia. All of this, I did utilizing the javascript language, either on the browser, either on the server.

It was an incredible experience to move to a different country, and work on different clients and projects. Through Aubay clients, I've met the companies EdirectInsure, and GoWithFlow.

GoWithFlow (Portugal) (current)

After some time as developer consultant allocated at GoWithFlow, they called me to work directly with them. I was hired as a senior software engineer to maintain, improve and scale a subset of their systems, specifically the frontend solutions, and guide junior and middle developers.

GoWithFlow, as described in their site (www.gowithflow.io) : "Formed officially as Flow in late 2019, when Portuguese energy giant Galp acquired a majority stake, the company today manages thousands of vehicles, EV charging stations and consumer end users across multiple software and services implementations in corporations and communities around the world."

At GoWithFlow, I learnt how to really work following a well designed development process, we follow SCRUM properly, we do CI/CD through gitlab, unit and integration testing as it is needed.

The most exciting solution I have worked on was to bring our big frontend into a microfrontend architecture. A lot of refactoring was done, a lot of coupling was removed, so that subsets of the system could be separated and placed per domain, and yet reuse same shared services. Angular's dependency injection system was the hero to achieve this, as different microservices needed different implementations of the same interface.

Venqueuer - https://github.com/vmvini/venqueuer

Even though we can achieve the same behavior through reducers and promises or callbacks, I like to remember this short library. I created it as a way of studying javascript.
An javascript asynchronous code enqueuer.
Useful for queue up repetitive tasks that should be executed in order, in a way that the end of one task calls the next in the queue.

ytComment - https://www.ytcomment.com/

This is a simple tool I created to help youtube addicteds like me to find specific comments in a specific video.
As I like to keep things simple, the frontend it is rendered by the server using the template render pug js.
